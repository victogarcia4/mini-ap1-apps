Hydrogen Bonds — the weak team-players

A hydrogen bond is a weak attraction between a slightly positive H (in a polar bond) and a slightly negative atom (O, N) on another molecule. Individually weak, but collectively powerful:
  • Give water its cohesion, surface tension & high boiling point.
  • Hold the two strands of DNA together (A=T, G≡C base pairs).
  • Stabilize protein folding (α-helix, β-sheet).
Educational model. Bond boundaries use the common teaching cutoffs: ΔEN < 0.4 nonpolar covalent, 0.4–1.7 polar covalent, > 1.7 ionic. Electronegativity values are Pauling-scale approximations.
