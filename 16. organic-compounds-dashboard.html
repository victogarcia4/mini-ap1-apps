Key Concepts

Most human enzymes work best at 37 °C and pH 7.35–7.45. Extreme heat or pH disrupts hydrogen bonds, denaturing the enzyme and destroying its active site.

Phase 4: The ATP Cycle

Toggle between hydrolysis and phosphorylation. Watch the terminal phosphate detach and energy release in real time.

ATP + H₂O → ADP + Pᵢ + ⚡ Energy (~7.3 kcal/mol)
Hydrolysis: ATP → ADP + Pᵢ releases ~7.3 kcal/mol. The terminal phosphoanhydride bond is broken, releasing energy for cellular work (muscle contraction, active transport, biosynthesis).
Phosphorylation: ADP + Pᵢ + energy → ATP. Performed by ATP synthase during cellular respiration (oxidative phosphorylation) or substrate-level phosphorylation in glycolysis/the Krebs cycle.
Reversibility: The ATP/ADP cycle is the cell's energy currency. ATP is continuously hydrolyzed and resynthesized — the average human recycles their body weight in ATP daily.
