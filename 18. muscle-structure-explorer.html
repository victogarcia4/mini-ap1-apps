Muscle Tissue Structure
Skeletal muscles are organs composed of muscle fibers (cells), connective tissue sheaths, blood vessels, and nerves. Use the tabs on the right to drill down from macroanatomy to molecular structures.

Muscle Fascicle (Bundle)

Internal bundle of muscle cells

Muscle Fiber (Cell)

Myofibril (Organelle)

Contractile cylinder running through the cell

Sarcomere (Functional Unit)

Contractile segment between Z-lines

Skeletal Muscle

An entire skeletal muscle acts as an organ, consisting of skeletal muscle fibers, connective tissue coverings, nerves, and blood vessels.

Scale: Entire Organ (Centimeters)
Key Components: Fascicles, Epimysium, Deep Fascia, Blood vessels

Connective Tissue Coverings

Click a connective sheath to view its location and binding properties:

Deep Fascia

Tough outer sheet surrounding muscles

Epimysium

Wraps the entire muscle belly

Perimysium

Wraps each muscle fascicle

Endomysium

Wraps each individual muscle cell

Tendon / Aponeurosis

Extensions that anchor muscle to bone

Deep Fascia

A sheet of dense connective tissue that surrounds the entire muscle and groups of muscles, separating them into compartments.

Layer: Outer Compartmental Cover
Primary Fiber Type: Dense irregular collagenous tissue

Sarcomere & Myofilaments

Click a filament or zone to explore molecular anatomy:

Thick Filament (Myosin)

Bundles of myosin with double globular heads

Thin Filament (Actin)

Twisted strands of actin containing binding sites

Tropomyosin

Regulatory band blocking myosin-binding sites

Troponin

Regulatory protein with calcium binding sites

Z-Line / Disc

Boundary anchor for thin filaments

A-Band (Dark Band)

Full length of the thick filaments

I-Band (Light Band)

Thin filaments only, crossing Z-lines

H-Zone

Thick filaments only, in center of A-band

M-Line

Protein line supporting thick filaments

Thick Filament (Myosin)

Composed of myosin molecules. The tails point toward the M-line while the heads form cross-bridges with actin active sites.

Main Protein: Myosin
Key feature: Binding sites for Actin and ATP
