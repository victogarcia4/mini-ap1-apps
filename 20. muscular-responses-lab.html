Isotonic vs. Isometric Contraction

Same Muscle, Two Different Jobs

Isotonic — muscle shortens, load moves

5 lb
Load moving ↑
At rest: the elbow is extended, the biceps is relaxed and at full length.

Isometric — muscle tenses, load doesn't move

No movement
At rest: the forearm presses lightly against an immovable wall.
Most everyday movements combine both: a muscle may tense isometrically to stabilize a joint (fixator action) while a neighboring muscle contracts isotonically to move a limb.

Fast-Twitch vs. Slow-Twitch Fibers

Two Fiber Types, Two Strategies

Slow-Twitch (Red / Type I)

ColorRed — rich in myoglobin
Blood supplyExtensive capillary network
MitochondriaNumerous
Main ATP sourceAerobic respiration
Contraction speedSlow to reach peak tension
Fatigue resistanceHigh — sustains long periods
Typical usePosture, marathon running

Fast-Twitch (White / Type II)

ColorWhite — low myoglobin
Blood supplySparse capillary network
MitochondriaFew
Main ATP sourceAnaerobic glycolysis
Contraction speedFast, powerful contraction
Fatigue resistanceLow — fatigues quickly
Typical useSprinting, jumping, heavy lifts
