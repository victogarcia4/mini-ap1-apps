Smooth & Cardiac Muscle
Smooth muscle lines organs and vessels; cardiac muscle powers the heart. Both are involuntary, but each has a distinct structure and contraction mechanism.

Two Types of Smooth Muscle

Compare to skeletal muscle: Skeletal fibers rely on a well-developed sarcoplasmic reticulum and troponin. Smooth muscle has a much smaller SR and no troponin — calcium instead activates calmodulin, which turns on MLCK to phosphorylate myosin.
