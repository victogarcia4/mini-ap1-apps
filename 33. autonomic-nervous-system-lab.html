Need-To-Know Rules

Rule: Somatic motor pathways use one neuron to skeletal muscle. Autonomic motor pathways usually use two neurons: preganglionic neuron, ganglion, then postganglionic neuron to the organ.

Division Snapshot

Fight-or-Flight vs. Rest-and-Digest

Sympathetic DivisionThoracolumbar
Mobilizes the body for activity, stress, exercise, and emergencies. Preganglionic cell bodies originate in the thoracic and lumbar spinal cord. Most postganglionic fibers release norepinephrine.
  • Short preganglionic fibers, long postganglionic fibers
  • Ganglia usually near the spinal cord
  • Can produce widespread body responses
Parasympathetic DivisionCraniosacral
Conserves energy and supports routine maintenance such as digestion, elimination, and recovery. Preganglionic neurons arise from the brainstem and sacral spinal cord. Postganglionic fibers release acetylcholine.
  • Long preganglionic fibers, short postganglionic fibers
  • Ganglia are in or near target organs
  • Usually produces localized organ responses
Reference image for the opposing autonomic effects on the pupil, heart, lungs, digestive tract, bladder, and reproductive organs.

Two-Neuron Chain

Preganglionic Neuron

Cell body in thoracic/lumbar spinal cord. Axon exits the CNS and releases ACh at a nearby ganglion.

Autonomic Ganglion

A synapse outside the CNS. Nicotinic receptors receive ACh from the preganglionic neuron.

Postganglionic Neuron

Long axon reaches smooth muscle, cardiac muscle, or glands. Usually releases norepinephrine.

Predict The Organ Response

Heart

Sympathetic

Increases heart rate and force of contraction to deliver more blood during stress or exercise.

Parasympathetic

Decreases heart rate during rest, mainly through the vagus nerve.

Memory cue: Sympathetic pushes output; parasympathetic slows the pace.

Case Calls

Case 1

A student hears a crash behind them. Pupils dilate, heart rate rises, bronchioles widen, and digestion slows.
