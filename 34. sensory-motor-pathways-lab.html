Need-To-Know Rules

Two-Way Communication

Body-to-Brain and Brain-to-Body Traffic

Ascending TractsSensory / Afferent
Ascending tracts conduct sensory impulses from body parts up toward the brain. In general, they are associated with posterior and lateral white matter columns and carry information such as touch, pressure, pain, temperature, and proprioceptive movement sense.
Descending TractsMotor / Efferent
Descending tracts conduct motor impulses from the brain down toward effectors. In general, they are associated with anterior and lateral white matter columns and influence skeletal muscle control, muscle tone, sweat glands, and posture.
