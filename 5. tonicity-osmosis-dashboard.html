Reference — Always-On Comparison

🎈 Imagine a water balloon in a pool…

Same water on both sides = balanced. The balloon stays the same. That's isotonic.

Pool water is fresher (less salty) than inside the balloon = water rushes IN, the balloon fills up and might POP. That's hypotonic.

Pool water is salty = water leaves the balloon, and it shrivels like a raisin. That's hypertonic.

Sneaky urea: it can walk straight through the balloon skin, so it never pulls water — a urea "pool" acts like plain fresh water!
